Temple Construction

A self-initiated woodworking project designed and built as a gift for my mom. Created to replace expensive, over-ornamented mass-produced temples with a simple, open, and sturdy design that fits our home decor and was built on a very low budget. I realized, temples sold in stores are structurally no different from tables with added decoration and I decided to design and build one myself. My goal was to create something sturdy, open, minimal, and functional rather than decorative. Working with basic materials and tools, I built the entire piece for only fifty dollars. The project pushed me to think critically about design efficiency, cost and material efficiency. A thoughtful design and efficient material use can replace expensive, mass-produced objects with something personal and intentional.

About Me

"I can always cut something off, but I can't add it back on" — that's the cornerstone of both engineering and my life.

I am Reyan Bhattacharjee. I'm a senior at West Windsor-Plainsboro High School South, planning to study Industrial Design or Engineering. I've completed NJIT's Summer Architecture and Portfolio Program and work as a lifeguard and swim instructor. I'm also an Eagle Scout and a maker who believes learning happens best when you build.
