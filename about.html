About Me

"I can always cut something off, but I can't add it back on" — that's the cornerstone of both engineering and my life.

I've learned to measure twice, build once, and make things work no matter what. Whether I'm welding, sketching, or salvaging materials, I'm happiest when I'm creating.

I'm currently a senior at West Windsor-Plainsboro High School South, planning to study Industrial Design or Engineering. I've completed NJIT's Summer Architecture and Portfolio Program and work as a lifeguard and swim instructor. I'm also an Eagle Scout and a maker who believes learning happens best when you build.

What I Do

Woodworking

Custom furniture, structures, and anything that needs to be built from lumber

Welding & Metalwork

MIG welding, metal fabrication, working with scrap steel and aluminum

Design & Sketching

Hand sketches and technical drawings to plan builds before cutting

Resourcefulness

Finding creative solutions with reclaimed and recycled materials

Background

I grew up in Plainsboro, NJ, and have been building things for as long as I can remember. My projects range from small-scale crafts to large structures, and I'm always looking for the next challenge. I believe in hands-on learning and making the most of available resources.
